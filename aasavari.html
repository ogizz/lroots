AASAVARI KAKNE

LAUNCHPAD’17 CONTESTANT NO.5 - AASAVARI KAKNE

Aasavari is a second year student pursuing her titulo de grado from IIT Delhi, Mathematics department. When not singing to herself (Which is mostly), Aasavari can be found reading books or travelling or shaking a leg or two. Being more oriented towards academics, she could not pay a fare amount of attention and time to music. Nevertheless, when she joined IITD, she performed at various events and won quite a few of them. Joining the Indian Music Society or IITD- Mridang was a whole new experience for her. The music society helped her improve and realize she can do a lot better.

By the end of her first year, Aasavari improved a lot and also won the Best Fresher Music award. This was a great start to her journey of music. Later she participated in the Eastern Group Song competition which is a part of IIT delhi’s annual cultural fest Rendezvous. This was a great learning experience for her. Now as the “Diro” of Mridang, the tables seem to have turned for Aasavari. She now guides the members of Mridang and they all practice together. She, through Mridang, wishes to promote the Indian music culture.

    • Having a beautiful voice is not enough, the knowledge of rhythms and ‘surs’ is important as well, Says Aasavari. She believes in having a never-give-up attitude. “I like focusing on one thing at a time and if something comes between me and my goals, I make sure I don’t let it stop me and I fight through it.” This aggression and passion that Aasavari hold in herself has got her a long way. But often we all face problems and some or the other aspect of our lives is affected. Taking up too many things at once and having her grades suffer due to that was one of the lowest points in Aasavari’s life. “It was difficult but I survived and learnt my lessons.”

    • The very name ‘Aasavari’ has music in it and hence answers a number of questions. Aasavari has a musical family background. From her grandfather to her sister, a lot of people learnt music and hence her inclination towards it. “I was eventually s attached to music, I can never let it go now.” Aasavari believes if one takes a certain hobby seriously and manages time efficiently, only then can he/she make something productive out of it. The cutting edge competition, she says, is one of the biggest struggles one faces and needs to fight through. Music, she says, demand a lot of your time and energy and if you give it that, it’s immensely rewarding. But at some point or the other one needs to prioritize between a couple of things and that’s when it becomes extremely confusing and difficult. Learning the pure classical forms, be it western or eastern, and sharpening you most brilliant self, is the secret to being a successful musician according to her. She ends with a note “Better version of you will be tomorrow, just keep practicing” Watch her video and if you like her voice, do vote for her here!
