LAUNCHPAD’17 CONTESTANT NO.4 - ASHISH PRATAP SINGH

“Where his colleagues are planning for a stable job, he has held hands with music. He knows it’s going to be tough but is ready to do whatever it takes.” With such passion, Ashish Pratap Singh, student of IIT Delhi wants to dedicate his whole life to music.

He is currently in his 4th year perusing B.tech in IT. His journey with music started in his 6th standard. He daily used to play cricket in the streets of korba, Chhattisgarh with his friends. One of his friends ‘the Bengali boy’, Manojit used to get the bat to play with. They didn’t play on Wednesdays. When asked by his father why he didn’t play on Wednesdays he replied that the Bengali boy didn’t turn up on those days. His father told him to find out what he did on those days. To his surprise he used to attend music classes. ” why not you too learn music” said his father. This ‘Mysterious Wednesday’ made him recognize the talent in him, the hidden singer in him. Pandey sir, was his first music teacher who made him familiar with the classical music. He learned classical for 2 years. “I enjoyed every bit of it” says he. Then further he learned harmonium and then guitar. He adored his journey and was unstoppable.

    • The major kick to his journey was when he came to IIT. His first inter hostel fest ‘DHWANI’ was a blunder. He forgot the lyrics in the middle of the song and stood there numb. He was depressed but when the judge announced the result his hostel stood third among 13 hostels. The judge was mesmerized by his voice. This made him believe in himself and his ability. The next year they stood 3rd again and then were winners twice in upcoming fests. This man opened an NGO in his 2nd year then went for a startup too. Nothing thrilled him so much as music did.

    • “Every time I sing I want to do better than before but I fail miserably, I fail daily.” says he. This failure give him the spark to become better each day. Mr. Singh’s ‘never give up attitude’ is truly mentionable. Whenever he feels low music helps him to come out of that zone. That’s what zeal is. That’s what true love is. The most memorable incident of his life was when his friend and he went to Banaras in December 2015 and composed a song on the glorifying river Ganges. “teri baat ke aage koi baat nahi” that’s how he described the strength of the river. “All I want to say is to look for a purpose in your music, nothing is more valuable than a purposeful life” with this, he signs off. Watch him sing and if you like his voice, vote for him here!
